Akihabara

By: Briana Patricio

What is Akihabara

Akihabara has gone through a major transformation in these past few decades. Akihabara was mainly known for its wide inventory of electrics, nowadays it’s known for its wide variety of Otaku culture. In the 2000’s Akihabara slowly turned into the anime center it is today. Akihabara went from mainly selling computers and console games to many services that appeal to the Otaku style such as manga and figure stores, themed cafes, and anime arcades.

Manga and Figure Heaven

One of the first things an Otaku would want to do in Akihabara is to go to an anime store and find all types of merchandise. Akihabara has a high variety of stores, some with manga or figures or even both. These stores have multiple floors of all the anime merch someone could want. Most stores have each floor categorized by genre, shows, or items. There’s one popular store called Animate Akihabara Main Building with seven floors with different manga, figures, and other anime merchandise. On their website, they have a layout of each floor ranging from Dengeki Bunko’s works to Love Live. Animate is one of the many stores like this in Akihabara.

Themed Cafes

Themed cafes are well known in Japan in general but Akihabara has a range of differences. Akihabara has animal cafes, anime cafes, maid cafes, and even vampire and ninja cafes. Anime themed and maid cafes embody Otaku culture. Many of these cafes have themed foods and interior decorations from the anime. A famous cafe in Akihabara is the Gundam Cafe. The front entrance is designed after the headpiece of the mechas and is followed by drawings of the mechas along the walls. The restaurant area is a remake of the dining hall in the anime and is covered with screens playing scenes from the show. While in Zeon's Diner Tokyo is another dining area but it resembles the principality of Zeon it also has interactive projection sheets as tables. There’s also a goods shop as well as a cafe that sells Gundam merchandise and custom Gundam themed drinks.

 

Anime Arcade

Akihabara has many arcades as well as stores. Sega Ge-Sen is one of the biggest arcades in Akihabara with six floors, two floors used for prize games such as claw machines and other skill games, and four floors used just for video games. The prize games have a variety of prizes you can win such as plushies, figures, posters, and other anime merchandise. Most of these games have chibi plushies of popular anime characters such as Rem from Re-Zero, the girls from Love Live, and Pokemon. As for figures and other merchandise, Sega Ge-San has many different types of anime items you can win. They have figures from Yu-Gi-Oh, Pokemon, Is the Order a Rabbit, Monogatari series, the Fate series, and Love Live. The other merchandise is mainly for shoujo animes like Love Live. For video games, it’s mainly action-based and fighting type games. The video games they have are Final Fantasy, Fate, Gundam, and Street Fighter. There’s also music-based video games. These games have a character for Love Life and other musical anime that help you with the game.
