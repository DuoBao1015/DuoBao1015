Japanese pop culture part 4：
Godzilla

Godzilla

Godzilla is one of the most well-known symbols in Japanese popular culture and the world. It first appeared in the 1954 movie Godzilla. Since then, Godzilla has become a worldwide pop culture symbol, starring in 31 movies, and also appeared in other media, including video games, novels, comics, and movies.

In the early works of "Godzilla", it was a symbol of fear and ferocity, and its nuclear-contaminated physique caused it to spread all anger on humans. Godzilla represents the fear of science and technology led by nuclear threats. Under the special circumstances of the Cold War and the haze of nuclear weapons, Godzilla became one of the main reasons Godzilla went to the world.

With the influence of technology and society, the positioning of the monster headed by Godzilla has also changed, from a frightening image to a diversified setting. Godzilla and King Kong are one of the typical representatives. They sometimes stand on the side of humans to protect humans.
