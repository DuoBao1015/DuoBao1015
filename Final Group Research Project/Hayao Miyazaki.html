Hayao Miyazaki

Hayao Miyazaki

Hayao Miyazaki is a Japanese animator, animator, manga artist, animation director, and animation screenwriter. Joined Toei Animation Company in 1963 as an animator. In 1971, he joined the "Insect Production Animation Department" established by Osamu Tezuka. In 1974, he joined Zuiyou Video and created "Girls in the Alps" with Takada Takada and Odabe Yoichi. In 1979, he transferred to the Tokyo Film News Agency and created his first film "Lupin III, the City of Cagliostro". He started to create manga independently in 1982, and serialized the manga "Nausicaa" on "Animage", which won the 23rd Japan Comic Artists Association Award. In 1984, he directed "Nausicaa", which won 4 awards including the Best Animated Short Film Award at the Rome Fantasy Film Festival. On November 8, 2014, he was awarded the Lifetime Achievement Award at the 87th Academy Awards.
