Japanese pop culture part 3：
kawaii

Kawaii

Originally, the word cute in Japanese was usually used for young and small people or things. If it is used on adults, it would be a little rude. However, as the times change, its usage has gradually become broader, and the objects used are no longer limited. Cuteness has become a national endorsement in Japan.

pikachu

Pikachu is a type of Pokémon that appeared in the handheld game series "Pokémon" released by Nintendo of Japan. For Pikachu's prototype, there has always been a big controversy. Mice, chipmunks, even chinchillas and rabbits are all speculated prototypes. In 1999, Pikachu was selected by "Time" as the second most influential person of the year, and was hailed as "the most beloved animated character after Hello Kitty". In 2003, with an income of 800 million US dollars, "Forbes" "Listed Pikachu as the most profitable virtual character of the year.

Hello_Kitty

Kitty cat was born in 1974, a famous Japanese cartoon star. She is an Englishman named Kitty White (Kitty White), Scorpio, third grade, has a pet cat named Charmmy Kitty, and a twin sister. Hello Kitty was given British ancestry because British culture was very popular with Japanese girls at that time. Kitty's name comes from a cat raised by Alice in the book Alice in the Mirror by Lewis Carroll. Hello Kitty’s hot era is when Japanese girls’ hearts and cuteness prevail. Hello Kitty conforms to this trend. Her audience is of all ages. She inspires women’s subconscious desire for protection and care. Memories of the times.

Kumamon

Kumamoto was originally designed as a mascot to bring more tourism and other additional income to Kumamoto Prefecture. In 2011, he was awarded the Minister of Sales and Happiness of Kumamoto Prefecture and became Japan's first mascot civil servant. While revitalizing the economy of Kumamoto Prefecture and promoting the reputation of Kumamoto Prefecture, Kumamoto Kuma, relying on its cute image and unique authorized operation method, has gained unimaginable welcome in Japan and beyond, and has become extremely popular in the world. Mascot.

Chibi Maruko-chan

"Chibi Maruko" is one of the world's most famous and influential animation works. Its animation has ranked among the top three in Japanese animation ratings for more than 20 consecutive years. By 2013, it has exceeded 1,000 episodes. It is still serialized and popular on Fuji TV every Sunday at 6 pm during prime time. It is in the hearts of Japanese men, women and children. National animation. This work is based on the author’s childhood life. The story revolves around Xiao Maruko and his family and classmates. It is about family, friendship, or some trivial things in life. Among them, there are smiles and tears, reminiscent of childhood childishness.

Crayon Shin-chan

"Crayon Shin-chan" is a manga created by Japanese manga artist Usui Yoshito. Mr. Usui Yoshito said that he created the image of Xiaoxin because when he observed his children, he found that children’s ideas are often so unique that the author is attracted to the world of children. All children are well-behaved and mischievous. This duality is very interesting to the author. Xiaoxin is a 5 year old boy who is in kindergarten. He is precocious and likes to admire and strike up a conversation with beautiful women. At first, Xiaoxin formed a three-person family with his father Guangzhi and his mother Miya. Later, the stray dog Xiaobai was added. The days are frequent and trivial but warm and moving.

Doraemon

The comic describes a cat-shaped robot from the 22nd century, Doraemon, who was entrusted by its owner, Ye Bi Se-soo, and returned to the 20th century, with the help of various future props taken out of the four-dimensional pocket to help Se-soo. Great grandfather, Nobi Nobita, a primary school student, resolves all kinds of difficult problems around him, as well as the easy, humorous and touching stories that happened in life and the friends around him. The creation of Doraemon dates back to a certain deadline in 1969.
